Thomas Edison

Edison's timeline

  • 1847 ~ Born in a small town of Milan, Ohio in the USA. His mother was a school teacher and his father was an exiled Canadian political firebrand.

  • 1866 ~ Worked for a year at Western Union. Was fired for spilling sulfuric acid when he was working with a battery.

  • 1869 ~ Developed his first invention, Universal Stock Printer which was an improved stock ticker.

  • 1871 ~ Marries her first wife, Mary Stilwell

  • 1876 ~ Opens first industrial research laboratory.

  • 1879 ~ Begins working to improve carbon filament lamp. He improves it to last up to 1200 hours.

  • 1880 ~ Patents electric Direct Current (DC).

  • 1882 ~ Nikola Tesla begins working for Edison. He tried to improve the electrical equipments using overseas knowledge.

  • 1884 ~ First wife dies.

  • 1885 ~ Nikola quits working for Tesla after some disagreements.

  • 1886 ~ Marries second wife, Mina Miller.

  • 1892 ~ Creates the company General Electric and patents two way telegraph.

  • 1915 ~ Accidently ignored by Nobel Prize committee

  • 1931 ~ Dies of complications of diabetes.
